10 Simple Ways to Lower Your Bill This December

  • Naerem Sawyer
  • Nov 20, 2025

As temperatures rise, keeping your home cool becomes a top priority. However, air conditioning and fans can quickly increase your energy consumption. Here at Midwest Light and Power, we want to help you stay comfortable without draining your wallet.

Here are our top tips for efficient energy usage during the hot season.

"The cheapest energy is the energy you don't use. Efficiency is the first step towards a sustainable and affordable energy future."

1. Optimize Your Air Conditioning

If you use an AC, setting the temperature just 2 degrees higher can save significantly on cooling costs. Ensure regular maintenance, such as cleaning filters, to keep the unit running efficiently.

2. Use Natural Ventilation

During the cooler parts of the day, like early morning or late evening, open windows to create a cross breeze. This pushes hot air out and lets fresh air in without using electricity.

3. Switch to LEDs

Old incandescent bulbs emit a lot of heat. Switching to LED bulbs keeps rooms cooler and uses up to 80% less energy for lighting.

4. Unplug Phantom Loads

Electronics that are plugged in but not in use still draw power. Unplug chargers, TVs, and microwaves when not in use, or use a smart power strip to cut the power automatically.

Implementing these small changes can lead to big savings on your next top-up. Check your daily usage on the Midwest App to see the difference these tips make!
